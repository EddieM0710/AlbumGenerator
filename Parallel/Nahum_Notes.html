THE PROPHECY OF NAHUM

chap. 1 ver. 8 Of the place thereof. . .Viz., of Ninive.

chap. 1 ver. 11 Shall come forth one, etc. . .Some understand this of Sennacherib. But as his attempt against the people seems to have been prior to the prophecy of Nahum, we may better understand it of Holofernes.

chap. 1 ver. 12 Though they were perfect, etc. . .That is, however strong or numerous their forces may be, they shall be cut off; and their prince or leader shall pass away and disappear.

chap. 1 ver. 14 Will give a commandment. . .That is, a decree, concerning thee, O king of Ninive, thy seed shall fail, etc.

chap. 1 ver. 15 Belial. . .The wicked one, viz., the Assyrian.

chap. 2 ver. 2 Hath rendered the pride of Jacob, etc. . .He hath punished Jacob for his pride; and therefore Ninive must not expect to escape. Or else, rendering the pride of Jacob means rewarding, that is, punishing Ninive for the pride they exercised against Jacob.

chap. 2 ver. 3 Of his mighty men, etc. . .He speaks of the Chaldeans and Medes sent to destroy Ninive.--Ibid. Stupefied. . .consopiti. That is, they drive on furiously like men intoxicated with wine.

chap. 2 ver. 5 Stumble in their march. . .By running hastily on.

chap. 3 ver. 8 Populous Alexandria. . .No-Ammon. A populous city of Egypt destroyed by the Chaldeans, and afterwards rebuilt by Alexander, and called Alexandria. Others suppose No-Ammon to be the same as Diospolis.

chap. 3 ver. 17 Locusts of locusts. . .The young locusts.

THE BOOK OF NEHEMIAS, WHICH IS CALLED THE SECOND OF ESDRAS

chap. 7 ver. 69 (Hitherto is related what was written in the record. From this place forward goeth on the history of Nehemias.)

chap. 7 ver. 70 Athersatha. . .That is, Nehemias; as appears from chap. 12. Either that he was so called at the court of the king of Persia, where he was cupbearer: or that, as some think, this name signifies governor; and he was at that time governor of Judea.

chap. 9 ver. 7 The fire of the Chaldeans. . .The city of Ur in Chaldea, the name of which signifies fire. Or out of the fire of the tribulations and temptations, to which he was there exposed.--The ancient Rabbins understood this literally, affirming that Abram was cast into the fire by the idolaters, and brought out by a miracle without any hurt.

chap. 9 ver. 17 And gave the head. . .That is, they set their head, or were bent to return to Egypt.

chap. 12 ver. 46 Sanctified. . .That is, they gave them that which by the law was set aside, and sanctified for their use.

chap. 13 ver. 4 Over this thing, etc. . .Or, he was faulty in this thing, or in this kind.
